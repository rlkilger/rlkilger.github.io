CEDAR HILLS

Location

Cedar Hills is located in the north-eastern area of Utah County. It rests close to the entrance of American Fork Canyon at the base of the Wasatch mountains, providing its residents with beautiful views of both the mountians to the east and the valley to the west. It is a quick 10 minute drive to I-15, making it a quick and easy commute to nearby cities like Provo and Salt Lake City.

History

Referred to as "The Bench," Cedar Hills became home to early settlers in the mid-1800s. For years, the area served as a well known turkey ranch that gained national publicity for its large turkeys and stunning mountain scenery. Named after the growth of cedar trees in the area, Cedar Hills was incorporated as a municipality on November 2, 1977 and became a city on August 3, 1999.

Demographics

Cedar Hills is a small town, but it has grown dramatically over the years and continues to grow quickly. According to the United States Census Bureau, as of 2019, Cedar Hills has a population of roughly 10,000 residents. In 2000, less than 20 years prior, the population was at just over 3000 people, meaning the population has more than tripled. The city is at an elevation of 4,957 ft, and the total surface area of Cedar Hills is 2.7 square miles.
